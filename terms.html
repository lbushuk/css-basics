Terms

In exchange for Indigenous title to their land, Treaty 6 provided:

  • An annual cash payment of $25 per chief
  • $15 per headman and $5 for all other band members
  • A one-time cash payment of $12 for each band member
  • reserve lands in the amount of one mile2 (about 2.5 km2) per family of five.
  • The establishment of schools on reserves
  • Twine and ammunition at a value of $1,500 per year
  • agricultural tools, such as gardening tools, livestock, horses and wagons.
  • $1,000 for Indigenous peoples farming on reserves, for the first three years after the treaties signing
  • A medicine chest on each reserve
  • Assistance in times of famine

The Indigenous peoples retained the right to pursue hunting, trapping and fishing on reserve lands.
